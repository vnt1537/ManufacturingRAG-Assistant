# Quality Inspection Checklist
Document ID: QIC-MH-204
Component Family: Servo Motor Housing Assemblies
Revision: B
Effective Date: 2026-02-26
Department: Quality Assurance
Approved By: L. Braun

## 1. Inspection Scope
This checklist applies to in-process and final inspection of MH-204-A servo motor housing assemblies produced on Assembly Line B.

## 2. Sampling Plan
- First article inspection: first 5 parts of each new batch
- In-process dimensional inspection: every 20th part
- Final visual inspection: 100 percent
- Torque verification audit: start of batch and every 2 hours

## 3. Required Inspection Tools
- Digital caliper
- Flatness gauge
- Surface roughness comparator
- Burr gauge
- Torque verification tool
- Inspection light

## 4. Dimensional Checks
### 4.1 Outer Diameter
Nominal: 120.0 mm
Tolerance: ± 0.1 mm
Result: Pass if value lies between 119.9 mm and 120.1 mm

### 4.2 Inner Bore Diameter
Nominal: 85.0 mm
Tolerance: ± 0.05 mm
Result: Pass if value lies between 84.95 mm and 85.05 mm

### 4.3 Overall Height
Nominal: 64.0 mm
Tolerance: ± 0.1 mm

### 4.4 Mounting Face Flatness
Maximum allowed: 0.08 mm
Reject if above limit

### 4.5 Burr Height on Sealing Edge
Maximum allowed: 0.05 mm
Reject if above limit

## 5. Surface and Cosmetic Criteria
### 5.1 Critical Surface Defects
The following are not permitted:
- cracks
- corrosion
- dents on sealing faces
- thread damage affecting screw engagement

### 5.2 Cosmetic Classifications
Class A surface:
Visible customer-facing surfaces under direct light at 50 cm distance

Class B surface:
Non-customer-facing but externally visible surfaces

Class C surface:
Internal or hidden surfaces after assembly

### 5.3 Scratch Limits
Class A: no scratches longer than 3 mm
Class B: no scratches longer than 8 mm and no width above 0.2 mm
Class C: scratches acceptable if functionality is unaffected

## 6. Assembly Verification
- Confirm sealing ring is present
- Confirm cover fit is flush
- Confirm no seal extrusion is visible
- Confirm 4 screws installed
- Confirm tightening torque record is available

## 7. Defect Classification
### Critical Defect
A defect that may affect fit, function, or safety.
Examples:
- thread damage
- cracked housing
- flatness above limit
- missing seal

### Major Defect
A defect that affects quality or appearance but not immediate safety.
Examples:
- visible seal extrusion
- wrong labeling
- repeated cosmetic defect on Class A surface

### Minor Defect
A defect with limited effect on appearance or usability.
Examples:
- light scratch within Class B acceptance
- minor contamination removable by cleaning

## 8. Disposition Rules
Critical defects: reject immediately and issue non-conformance report
Major defects: hold for quality review
Minor defects: rework if feasible, otherwise accept with approval

## 9. Traceability
Each inspected batch shall include:
- batch number
- operator ID
- inspector ID
- time stamp
- tool ID where applicable
