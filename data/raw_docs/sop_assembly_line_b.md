# Standard Operating Procedure
Document ID: SOP-ALB-017
Process: Servo Motor Housing Assembly
Line: Assembly Line B
Revision: A
Effective Date: 2026-02-20
Owner: Production Engineering
Approved By: M. Keller

## 1. Purpose
This procedure defines the standard assembly process for the MH-204-A servo motor housing and cover assembly on Assembly Line B.

## 2. Required Equipment
- Electric torque screwdriver with calibrated M6 program
- Pneumatic air gun for part cleaning
- Digital caliper
- Surface cleanliness lamp
- Batch traveler form
- Approved cleaning cloths

## 3. Required Components
- 1 × Servo motor housing body, Part No. MH-204-A
- 1 × Housing cover, Part No. HC-204-B
- 4 × M6 stainless steel screws
- 1 × Sealing ring, Part No. SR-85-NBR
- Approved assembly grease, Type AG-4

## 4. Safety Requirements
Operators shall wear cut-resistant gloves and safety glasses at all times.
Compressed air shall not be directed toward skin or clothing.
Any damaged tools shall be removed from service immediately.

## 5. Pre-Assembly Checks
Verify that the housing part number matches the batch traveler.
Verify that the housing revision is approved for current production release.
Check that no visible chips, dents, corrosion, or contamination are present.
Check that burr height on sealing edges does not exceed 0.05 mm.
Verify calibration label on torque screwdriver is valid.

## 6. Assembly Steps
### Step 1: Cleaning
Blow off loose particles from the housing and cover using compressed air.
Wipe the sealing face with an approved cleaning cloth.
Do not use solvents unless approved by production engineering.

### Step 2: Seal Placement
Install the sealing ring SR-85-NBR into the designated groove.
Ensure the seal is not twisted or pinched.
Apply a thin continuous film of AG-4 grease to the seal contact area.

### Step 3: Cover Positioning
Place the housing cover HC-204-B onto the housing body.
Align all four screw holes before inserting screws.

### Step 4: Screw Insertion
Insert all four M6 screws by hand for at least two turns.
Do not fully tighten one screw before all screws are seated.

### Step 5: Torque Tightening
Tighten screws in a diagonal cross pattern.
Program setting: 10.0 Nm nominal torque.
Acceptable range: 9.5 to 10.5 Nm.
If any screw measures below 9.5 Nm or above 10.5 Nm, the assembly shall be reworked and rechecked.

### Step 6: Visual Inspection
Verify flush cover fit around the full perimeter.
Check for visible seal extrusion.
Check that no scratches deeper than cosmetic acceptance level are introduced during assembly.

## 7. In-Process Verification
First 3 assemblies of each shift shall be verified by the line leader.
Torque verification shall be recorded at batch start and at 2-hour intervals.
If two consecutive torque failures occur, production shall be stopped and the tool shall be recalibrated.

## 8. Escalation Rules
Escalate immediately to quality if:
- thread damage is detected
- seal extrusion is visible after tightening
- repeated torque failure occurs
- incorrect revision part is found in production

## 9. Records
The following records shall be maintained:
- Batch traveler
- Torque verification log
- Rework record, if applicable
- Non-conformance report, if applicable
